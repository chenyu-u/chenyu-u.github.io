01 — About

The Engineer
Behind the Work

I'm Chenyu Li, a second-year Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Toronto in the BASc + PEY Co-op program, with a Robotics & Mechatronics Minor, Business Minor, and EV Design Certificate on the go.

I like making things that actually work — modelling in SolidWorks, validating in ANSYS, printing prototypes, and then seeing how they hold up in the real world. That process took me from Calgary to the Netherlands this past year to compete at the International Small Wind Turbine Competition.

Before university I ran a landscaping business from age 14, which turned out to be a better education in managing people, schedules, and equipment than most things I've done since. I speak English and Mandarin natively and get by in German.

04 — Life Outside Engineering

What I Do
For Fun

The best engineers are well-rounded people. Here's what I love to do in my free time outside of studying and working on projects.

Skiing

Grew up skiing in Banff since nothing beats a powder day in the Rockies. I'm hoping to ski tour the Alps someday.

Hiking

Just like skiing, most of my best hikes have been in Banff, the kind of trails where you're too tired to think about anything else. A good way to reset between school terms.

Golf

I picked up golf during my senior year of high school and have been itching to play ever since. I'm always up for a casual round with friends or family when the weather's good. Still working on keeping it out of the trees.

Running

My go-to way to clear my head and think about new ideas. No goal in particular, I just love running, espeically downtown along the coast of Lake Ontario.

Travelling

I travel to see how things are built. The engineering, the history, and the different ways people have solved the same problem is always fascinating. Every city has its own answer to how infrastructure, technology, and public life should work, and it's hard not to notice that once you start looking. The food is always a plus as well!

🇪🇸 Spain 🇩🇪 Germany 🇨🇿 Czechia 🇳🇱 Netherlands

Hockey

Started playing club hockey at 6 and never really stopped — still get out to rip pucks whenever I can, over a decade in now. I've done plenty of coaching too, privately and as a volunteer with two U11 teams for Knights Hockey Club through high school, which turned out to be a great way to practice explaining things clearly and adjusting on the fly.
